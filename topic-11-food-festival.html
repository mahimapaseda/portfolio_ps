Planning Process

Comprehensive planning approach for successful event execution

Strategic Planning

The planning process was started two weeks in advance. The first part was a meeting to determine the menu items based on popularity, ability to prepare, and cost. Once the menu items were finalized, our group decided to name the stall as "දුන්නම කන්නං කාලා දුවන්නම්", a lighthearted name that reflected both the theme and the products we offered.

Key Parts of the Planning Process:

  • Menu Selection: We decided achcharu, potato chips, and smoothies were the items we wanted to offer, taking into consideration the demand, profitability, and preparation time.
  • Market Research: Checked current prices for the items needed and found vendors who offered the best price.
  • Team Responsibilities: We allocated team members for purchasing, making, marketing, and managing the stall.
  • Marketing Strategy: Four days prior to the event, through social media and word of mouth, we published posts to generate excitement and interest in our products.
  • Procurement Planning: The distance of members from the event affected buying and purchasing. Team members who lived far bought ingredients and packaging ahead of time to bring on the day of the event.
  • Food Preparation: The achcharu and chips were prepared fresh on the event day and the day before the event. The smoothies were prepared on the day of the event for freshness.
  • Logistics Management: We organized containers, cups, and utensils so that we had them, and that packaging was organized for efficient product selling.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

Real-world insights from event management experience

Challenges Faced

  • Space Management: The toughest challenge was space management, since we have a variety of different types of achcharu and smoothies the provided table was not very efficient although we handled it very well.
  • Decision Making: During the planning process, since there was a pool of ideas deciding on the selling item took quite some time.
  • Quality Control: The cups that were purchased were not up to our expectations in size as well as quality.
  • Transportation: We prepared the smoothies in a team member's accommodation nearby. This required extra effort to transport them and ensure freshness and retain cooling of the smoothies.

Valuable Lessons Learned

  • Customer Diversity: During the sales, we observed that not all customers have the same need or want, also they are different in attitude as well. Although, some customers greeted us with positivity, there were a few customers who gave negative comments in regard to the worth they get for the money they pay.
  • Planning Importance: Having a prior sketch of the plan makes things easier and definitely hassle free.
  • Simplicity: Simple is better. Our food items did not require a hefty effort as some of the other stall's. It was just fruits and vegetables that we utilized to gain a profit.
  • Team Bonding: This might not be considered a lesson. But we became closer as friends and got to understand everyone better.

Event Execution

Successful implementation and day-of-event management

Event Day Success

The food festival was held on 29th of July and started at 12:30 PM in the ground floor of BOC Merchant Tower. We set up our stall, which was in close proximity to the back entrance, so the visitors saw the stall as they entered the building.

Key Execution Points:

  • Setup: We were able to begin setting up the stall by 11:00 AM, and prepared the tables and display, along with decorations, before the commencement of the event.
  • Product Arrangement: All products (achcharu, potato chips, and smoothies) were arranged in a creative manner. Potato chips were in bags beforehand for speedy purchases.
  • Peak Sales: The lunch break was one of the most active selling times, with the most people present at around 1:00 PM.
  • Popular Items: Achcharu was the fastest-selling item, and all of the initial stock sold out quickly, which led to previous customers coming back later in the day, hoping to purchase again.
  • Sales Tracking: All sales were tracked the entire time, so that we could keep a record.
  • Location Advantage: The stall was positioned perfectly at the entrance, so there was a lot of foot traffic.
  • Team Efficiency: The team operated in a seamless manner, and ensured a speedy delivery, despite the busy times.
  • Marketing Success: Our marketing step prior to the event made people curious and got some people to come to the event, which was a successful presence!
