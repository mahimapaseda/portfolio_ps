02. CV and Cover Letter Writing

Master the art of creating compelling CVs and cover letters that effectively showcase your qualifications, experience, and potential to prospective employers.

CV Writing Essentials

Personal Information

Include your full name, contact details, professional email, and LinkedIn profile. Keep it concise and professional.

Professional Summary

Write a compelling 2-3 sentence summary highlighting your key skills, experience, and career objectives.

Education & Qualifications

List your educational background, certifications, and relevant qualifications in reverse chronological order.

Work Experience

Detail your professional experience with specific achievements, responsibilities, and quantifiable results.

Skills & Competencies

Highlight technical skills, soft skills, and industry-specific competencies relevant to the target role.

Achievements & Awards

Showcase notable accomplishments, recognitions, and awards that demonstrate your value and impact.

CV Writing Best Practices

Keep It Concise

Limit your CV to 1-2 pages maximum. Recruiters spend only 6 seconds scanning CVs initially.

Use Keywords

Include relevant industry keywords and phrases that match the job description to pass ATS systems.

Quantify Achievements

Use numbers, percentages, and metrics to demonstrate the impact of your work and accomplishments.

Tailor for Each Role

Customize your CV for each application, emphasizing relevant experience and skills for the specific position.

Proofread Thoroughly

Check for spelling, grammar, and formatting errors. Consider having someone else review your CV.

Professional Formatting

Use consistent fonts, bullet points, and spacing. Keep the design clean and easy to read.

Reverse Chronological Order

List your most recent experience first. This helps recruiters quickly see your current level and progression.

Include Relevant Links

Add your LinkedIn profile, portfolio website, or GitHub profile if relevant to your field.

Cover Letter Writing

Cover Letter Structure

  • 1
    Header & Greeting

    Include your contact information and address the letter to the hiring manager by name when possible.

  • 2
    Opening Paragraph

    State the position you're applying for and how you learned about the opportunity. Grab attention with a strong opening.

  • 3
    Body Paragraphs

    Explain why you're interested in the role and company. Highlight relevant experience and achievements that match the job requirements.

  • 4
    Closing Paragraph

    Express enthusiasm for the opportunity, request an interview, and thank the reader for their consideration.

  • 5
    Professional Closing

    End with "Sincerely" or "Best regards" followed by your full name and signature.

Cover Letter Tips

Be Specific

Reference specific aspects of the company and role to show genuine interest and research.

Show Enthusiasm

Demonstrate genuine passion for the role and company culture while maintaining professionalism.

Connect to Company Values

Align your experience and values with the company's mission and culture.

Keep It Concise

Limit your cover letter to one page with 3-4 paragraphs maximum.

Key Learning Outcomes

Professional CV Creation

Develop the ability to create compelling, well-structured CVs that effectively showcase qualifications and experience.

Persuasive Cover Letters

Master the art of writing engaging cover letters that complement your CV and demonstrate your fit for specific roles.

Application Customization

Learn to tailor both CVs and cover letters for specific job applications to maximize your chances of success.
